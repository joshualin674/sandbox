Mission Statement:

The student architect exists in a precursory realm to that of architectural practice. Often exempt from the boundary of the “realistic,” the limits of creation become those of one’s imagination. Limitless creation - testing, adapting, and subsequent learning without risk of failure. The student architects build in a sandbox.

Sandbox is a student-sourced and operated architectural publication providing a platform for emerging student perspectives and explorations. Deepened through guidance from faculty advisors, it dismantles the notion that a developing architectural voice is one unworthy of broadcast, creating exploratory amateur architectural discourse in the present, as well as a referential future time capsule of frozen moments in constantly evolving architectural careers.

Written, designed, compiled, and edited by students from the School of Architecture, Sandbox releases works digitally through semester-long thematic issue cycles with a limited print format release to end each. Content within issues includes but is not limited to written, audio, and visual media, practitioner interviews, and student work, produced both in-house and from open calls-for-submissions.

Through recognizing the significance within sufficiency, Sandbox strives to add the diverse, undomesticated voice of the student to the conversation of architecture.

Audience:

Sandbox calls upon students, educators, designers, artists, and practitioners as both recipients and contributors in an open and equal discourse. Submissions from outside the Georgia Tech School of Architecture are welcomed and encouraged, further broadening the scope of discussions.

At the same time, Sandbox heavily emphasizes the role of the student as both a key contributor and consumer: The student plays a key role in diversifying the individuals that comprise architectural/design discourse. Sandbox facilitates discourse between all designers and artists regardless of title, professional experience, or grade level.

Interfaces:

As the primary interface, the SANDBOX website will allow for a place to publish and display work. The website houses new releases in full, as well as an archive of all prior issues/works. This digital format will ensure that students and faculty have easy access and clear direction when trying to find articles and/or other work.

As the secondary interface, Instagram (@sandbox.gt) will act as the main link between the publication and student body. Here we will release issue-specific abstracts/calls for submission at the beginning of each issue cycle, post updates, graphics, and snippets of work.
